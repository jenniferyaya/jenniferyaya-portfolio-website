Seated and motionless, I am staring into the red sky. The train humming along its route. Staring into the messy red sun splattered across the sky, I hope that it will envelope me whole. Killing all that is old and useless, imbuing me with everything that makes one special. And new.

Lansdowne Avenue: Closeup view of a residential building. Enjoying a conversation with and older gentleman and his silent companion at a tiny local run coffee shop, serving strong coffee and functional decor, he remarks that I am not from here. He knows this because, he knows everyone, having lived here long before all of these tall buildings where erected.

The train keeps forward, further west, never catching up. The space between me and that setting star keeps expanding. I am consumed by that space.

Chinatown, Downtown Toronto: A curious woman stands at the busy intersection. An unmoving statue, she stares into space. A meditation on some thing only found here, at this particular point in time.
