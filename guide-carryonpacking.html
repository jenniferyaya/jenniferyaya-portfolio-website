The Ultimate Carry-on Pakcing List

For trips one week or more

Europe is charming for its history and architecture and generally, the beautiful apartments are old, beautiful and not equipped with elevators. On my last overseas trip, my friend and I packed our bags for 10 days in France. Checking into our fifth story apartment was an adventure, from climbing the winding stairs to not being able to work the door knob, but it wasn't difficult because I only had my carry on luggaGe and a backpack with me. On the other hand, it took my friend a concerted 20 minute effort of pulling up the big piece of armor, erm luggage, onto each stair.

Packing light just makes sense! I always do a lot of research and planning before trips, and so, I want to share some of my carry-on packing tips, specifically for packing light. I’ve organized the packing list into six categories: luggage, documents, photocopies, electronics, clothing, toiletries, and extras. Packing light and not being cumbered by things is how I like to organize my life, so this is a philosophy I adhere to when traveling and customize it when dictated by a trip.

Luggage:

Carry on luggae

The Small Trade Luggage is my super! It has a combination lock, a retractable handle, and multi-direction wheels

Personal bag

I used my Fjäll Räven 15-inch Laptop bag

Packing cubes

Incredibly helpful, buy a good set on Amazon

An extra lock

Just in case for extra security, a small lock is nice to have

Documents:

Passport

For assurance, make sure that your passport is valid for six months beyond the end of your trip

Local ID

Again for assurance, bring an extra piece of identification

Extra Passport-sized pictures

In case you apply for metro passes and some countries will actually require an passport photos on entry

Pocket Cash, Debit, and Credit Cards

Photocopies of your passport and various IDs

Itinerary and other details

Any notes of places, train routes, addresses and phone numbers you need to keep close

Electronics:

Universal Adapter

Powerstrip

A power strip is useful for charging all of your devices

Phone, earbuds and charger

Laptop and charger

Clothing:

4/5 tops

Ideally take a few neutrals and a couple of dressier ones

2-4 bottoms

4-6 pairs of sock / 4-6 pairs of underwear / 3 bras

1 pair of workout leggings or shorts

1 jacket/coat

1 warm sweater/cardigan

2 pairs of shoes

One pair of comfortable, walking sneakers and a fancier pair of shoe

1 pair of pajamas

Toiletries:

Toothbrush, toothpaste, face wash

Makeup

Nars Velvet Matte Foundation Stick / Avène Lip Balm with Cold Cream / Houglass Arch Brow Volumizing Fiber Gel / Bite Beauty Multistick in Cerise

Since you can only pack maximum 3.4 ounces of liquids in your carry on, this is an area that I pare down ruthlessly. Other basic toiletries, I buy travel sized toiletries at the destination

Extras:

Pens, notebook or journal
